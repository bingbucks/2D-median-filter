What can you use a 2 Dimensional median filter for?

You can use a 2 dimensional median filter to remove impulsive noise from images, like this one shown here.

  • This image is barely recognizable with impulsive noise corruption. Only a non-linear filter, such as a median filter, could remove this noise completely. A linear filter would fail to remove the corrupt points without altering the image in an unacceptable manner.
  • The problem with a median filter, as with any image processing algorithms, is that there is a danger of inefficiencies and lack of speed because of the number of operations that must be performed. My algorithm uses a method suggested by Professor Dietz referred to as a Sliding Window Median Filter with linked lists. I highlight linked lists because they are the key to the algorithm, and the reason why my algorithm can beat MathWorks Matlab algorithm upto several times less. And later on, I will explain what linked lists are and how they are more efficient.
  • The goal for this design was SPEED, and of course, accuracy, both of which I accomplished. Though due to time constraints, I was not able to accomplish implementing the heart of Professor Dietz's thesis and that was a STM (Standard Type-M) Median Filter. And I'll go into a little detail about this.
